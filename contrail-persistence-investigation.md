## Plain-language summary

When a jet flies overhead at altitude, the white line behind it is a **contrail** — "condensation trail." A widespread online belief is that *real* contrails disappear in seconds and that long-lived trails spreading into cloud-like sheets must be something else: covert aerial spraying ("chemtrails"). This piece checks whether that belief survives contact with the historical and scientific record.

**The plain answer:** No. Persistent contrails that spread into cirrus-like ice cloud were described in eyewitness reports and analysed in scientific journals starting in **1918–1919** *(traced)* — half a century before commercial jet travel and 80 years before the chemtrails idea entered public discussion in 1999 *(traced)*. The basic physics was worked out by 1941–1953 *(traced)* and is still the basis of US Air Force contrail forecasting *(traced via Air Force Weather Agency operational doctrine)*.

**Why some contrails vanish in seconds and others last for hours** *(deferred to consensus: Schmidt 1941, Appleman 1953, Schumann 1996, IPCC AR6 Ch. 7)*:

- Jet engines burn kerosene; combustion releases hot water vapour.
- At cruise altitude (typically below −40 °C) the vapour can briefly reach water saturation and freeze onto soot/sulphate nuclei — the white line you see.
- What happens *next* depends on the **humidity of the surrounding air**.
- Dry air → ice crystals sublimate → trail vanishes in seconds.
- Ice-supersaturated air → crystals are stable and grow → trail can persist for hours and spread over thousands of km².
- Ice-supersaturated patches occur in roughly **10–45 %** of the upper troposphere at the latitudes where most aviation happens *(traced to IAGOS/MOZAIC in-situ observations; the previously-cited "10–20 %" figure is the conservative end of the range)*.

The same jet, on the same day, can produce a 10-second trail and a 6-hour trail in different patches of air. Both are "normal."

**What the strongest chemtrails case actually rests on**, stated fairly *(all four below are real documents — but each is a proposal, research paper, or course material, not deployment evidence)*:

- The Welsbach stratospheric-seeding patent (US 5,003,186 — Chang & Shih, Hughes Aircraft → Raytheon, filed 1990, issued 1991, expired 2010) *(traced)*.
- Edward Teller, "The Planet Needs a Sunscreen" (WSJ 17 Oct 1997, reprinted Hoover Institution 1998) *(traced)*.
- USAF Air University's "Weather as a Force Multiplier: Owning the Weather in 2025" (1996 Air Command and Staff College research paper, with explicit disclaimer that it doesn't represent USAF policy) *(traced via Internet Archive)*.
- The 1990 USAF Academy "Chemistry 131" course materials informally titled "Chemtrails" (a pun on Chemistry + Contrails for a freshman chemistry course; not a covert-program manual) *(traced via Metabunk and the Internet Archive scan)*.
- Soil samples allegedly showing elevated aluminium near "spraying events" *(memory — unverified for individual samples; the "What in the World Are They Spraying?" 2010 documentary cites these but Shearer et al. 2016 surveyed atmospheric-deposition experts who described the sampling methodology as known to produce contamination)*.

These establish that *proposals, patents, and research papers* about atmospheric intervention exist. They do not establish that any such program has been deployed.

**Why the case for chemtrails doesn't survive scrutiny:**

- Persistent contrails were documented in scientific journals from 1919 onward, decades before any plausible covert program *(traced)*.
- Allied bombers in WWII produced industrial-scale persistent contrails over Europe, measurable in weather-station temperature records of the time *(traced to Ryan, MacKenzie, Watkins & Timmis 2011, Int. J. Climatology, DOI 10.1002/joc.2392)*.
- Aluminium levels cited as alarming in chemtrail-advocate samples (~375 mg/kg in soil) are far below the natural crustal background (~82,000 mg/kg average upper continental crust; typical soils 10,000–300,000 mg/kg) *(traced to USGS and CDC ATSDR)*.
- The Shearer et al. 2016 expert survey (n = 77 contrail and deposition specialists) returned 76/77 (98.7 %) reporting no SLAP evidence, and 100 % rejected proponents' photo interpretations *(traced)*.
- No defector or supply-chain leak has emerged in 27 years from the global logistics chain a covert program would require *(this is an inductive argument, not proof; flagged)*.
- The financial incentives run *opposite* the usual conspiracy framing: aviation industry would prefer the chemtrails story (because contrail cirrus is now ~57 % of aviation's total ERF), yet industry-and-aerospace-funded research (DLR, ICAO CAEP) keeps confirming the science anyway *(traced)*.

**The honest bottom line:**

The claim "normal contrails can't persist" has no source in atmospheric science. It was first asserted in January 1999 by William Thomas in "Mystery Contrails May Be Modifying Weather" (Environmental News Service, syndicated 8 Jan 1999), citing no pre-1990 atmospheric-physics literature *(traced)*. The mainstream explanation — persistence depends on ambient humidity — was already standard textbook material *(traced to Appleman 1953, used operationally by USAF Air Weather Service)*.

A robust skeptical case would need: (i) a pre-1990 source showing aircraft contrails *never* persisted — none exists; the opposite exists in abundance; (ii) blinded, independently-replicated chemical analysis showing trail particulates inconsistent with kerosene combustion and ambient ice condensation — not produced in 27 years; (iii) a defector or supply-chain leak from the alleged global program — none in 27 years. Until one of these appears, the claim stays where the evidence puts it: **refuted**.

## The Physics (in brief)

**Schmidt–Appleman Criterion (SAC)** *(deferred to consensus — Schmidt 1941, Appleman 1953, Schumann 1996)*: A contrail forms when the mixing line between hot, water-vapour-rich engine exhaust and cold ambient air briefly crosses the liquid-water saturation curve. Below approximately −39 to −54 °C (engine and humidity dependent) this happens routinely at cruise altitude.

**Persistence vs. dissipation — ambient ice supersaturation (RHi)** *(traced)*:

- **RHi < 100 %:** ice crystals sublimate; trail dissipates in seconds to minutes.
- **RHi ≥ 100 % (ice-supersaturated regions, ISSRs):** crystals are thermodynamically stable, grow by vapour deposition, and the contrail **persists for hours and can spread**.

ISSRs cover roughly **10–45 %** of the upper troposphere in midlatitudes *(traced — IAGOS/MOZAIC long-term in-situ measurements; figure depends on season, region, and definition. The earlier "10–20 %" was the conservative end)*. Persistent contrails typically last 4–6 hours; satellite-tracked clusters up to ~14 hours; individual systems can cover >10,000 km².

**Climate impact — peer-reviewed consensus** *(deferred to consensus, with funding network noted below)*:
- Burkhardt & Kärcher 2011, *Nature Climate Change* 1:54–58 — first global simulation of contrail cirrus radiative forcing. Authors: DLR (German Aerospace Center) *(traced; paywalled — value of ~+38 mW m⁻² adjusted RF reported via independent citations)*.
- Lee et al. 2021, *Atmospheric Environment* 244:117834, DOI 10.1016/j.atmosenv.2020.117834 — contrail cirrus ERF = +57.4 mW m⁻² [17, 98] (~57 % of total aviation ERF) *(traced via ADS, ScienceDirect citation page, and ICAO CAEP reports; full PDF paywalled)*.
- IPCC AR6 WG1 Ch.7 adopts these values *(deferred to consensus)*.

## First-Principles Rebuild

**Decomposition:**
- C1: Contrails are caused by combustion-water-vapour condensation → **Bedrock** (thermodynamics).
- C2: Their behaviour depends only on engine output → **Refuted** — depends on both engine output *and* ambient atmosphere.
- C3: "Normal" atmosphere is dry at cruise altitude → **Assumption / false** — 20–45 % of upper-troposphere midlatitude air is ice-supersaturated.

**Verdict:** **Overturned.** The claim survives only by treating one specific atmospheric regime (dry upper troposphere) as the universal "normal" — a definitional smuggle.

## Bias self-audit (CLAUDE.md requirement)

**Would I have reached the same verdict if the politically/socially expected answer were reversed?**

Yes, on the central claim. The test applied was source-tracing, which is symmetric. Hypothesis A's claim ("persistent contrails exist") survives because it is anchored in 1918-era primary sources from before any plausible cover-up, and because the mechanism was established by a USAF military researcher (Appleman 1953) whose institutional pressure ran toward *operational* contrail prediction (for evasion), not climate advocacy. Hypothesis B's central claim ("contrails dissipate in seconds") fails because it is unsourced in the scientific record at any date.

**Where I was almost-but-not-quite-wrong:**
- The previous draft cited "10–20 %" ISSR prevalence; IAGOS data show the realistic upper bound is closer to 45 %. Underestimating the prevalence would (counter-intuitively) work in favour of Hypothesis B by making persistent contrails seem rarer. Corrected.
- The previous draft did not flag the **DLR institutional concentration** in the quantitative ERF synthesis. This is a legitimate independence concern and is now flagged with a downgraded warrant `(deferred to consensus, partial independence caveat)`.
- The previous draft characterised the USAF Academy "Chemistry 131" textbook as "mis-cited" with no explanation. The honest characterisation is: it's real, it's titled "Chemtrails," but it's a freshman chemistry course (the title is a pun); calling it merely "mis-cited" was thin. Corrected.
- The William Thomas article title is "Mystery Contrails May Be Modifying Weather" (8 Jan 1999), not what earlier secondary sources sometimes shorten to "Chemtrails – U.S. Military…" (April 1999). Corrected.
- The Ryan paper is 2011 (online), not 2012; the citation has been updated.

**Where uncertainty remains:**
- Quantitative ERF values rest on synthesis with DLR overlap. The qualitative claim (persistent contrails exist, have climate impact) is robust; the exact +57.4 mW m⁻² is not as independent as the field's citation patterns suggest.
- The "perfect cover-up" inductive argument against H2 (no whistleblower in 27 years) is suggestive, not conclusive. It is presented as one element among many.

## What Would Change This

Three things would force re-opening the question:

1. A pre-1990 contemporary source showing aviation-era trails *never* persisted. (None exists; the opposite exists in abundance.)
2. Blinded, independently-replicated chemical analysis of trail particulates returning elements/concentrations inconsistent with kerosene combustion + ambient ice condensation, with full chain-of-custody.
3. A defector / supply-chain leak from the alleged global program with documentary corroboration.

None of the three has materialised in the 27 years since the claim entered public discourse.
